# Puzzle Families

These families are reusable content grammars, not just loose themes.

## Archive Rooms

Ancient rooms of inscriptions, levers, sequences, sockets, witness marks, and symbolic machinery.

Use them to teach:

- pattern recognition
- ordering
- threshold logic
- visible motion versus meaningful progress

Desired feeling:
A room can seem active before it becomes legible.

## Garden of Growth

Processes of planting, tending, pruning, waiting, watching, and overgrowth.

Use them to dramatize:

- nurture versus interference
- bloom versus entanglement
- convergence versus proliferation
- patience as judgment, not passivity

Desired feeling:
The player should ask whether continued care will produce meaning or only deeper involvement.

## Echoing Paths

Labyrinthine, folded, mirrored, or graph-like routes that return in altered form.

Use them to dramatize:

- structure versus recurrence
- commitment under uncertainty
- effort allocation across threads
- altered return rather than simple repetition

Desired feeling:
The player should ask whether they are traversing a structure or revisiting it in another costume.

## Oracles

Interfaces that demand commitment before revelation.

Use them to dramatize:

- foresight as debt
- revelation as costly
- prediction without certainty
- the seduction of overreaching for clarity

Desired feeling:
The player should feel they bought a dangerous amount of future knowledge at a real price.

## Contradictory Relics

Objects that resist or invert the player's overlearned expectations.

Use them to dramatize:

- method failure
- local success becoming universal error
- hidden exception that still feels lawful

Desired feeling:
The world is not cheating; the world is responding at a deeper level than the player's shortcut.

## Mirror Chambers

Spaces that quote earlier puzzles, earlier notes, earlier reasoning, or earlier player habits.

Use them to dramatize:

- self-reference
- inheritance
- methods turning back on the player
- representation becoming active

Desired feeling:
The player is no longer entirely outside the puzzle space.

## The Unfinished Engine

Late-game megastructure made of understandable subsystems that never collapses into one universal rule.

Use it to dramatize:

- system-scale stewardship
- locality versus totality
- composition without final mastery
- legacy rather than victory

Desired feeling:
The player understands parts deeply while accepting that the whole is not reducible to a master key.

## Authoring rule

Every puzzle family should be expressible in the content system with:

- a visible surface
- a hidden process state
- a costed command set
- partial feedback
- one or more closure states
- one or more contradiction opportunities
